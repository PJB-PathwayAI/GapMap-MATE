## Purpose

The Capability Interpretation Engine exists to identify, interpret and explain capability hidden within military experience, qualifications, responsibilities, behaviours and experiences.

Its purpose is **not** to translate military terminology.
Its purpose is **not** to score people.
Its purpose is **not** to certify competence.

Its purpose is to help individuals understand what they have actually done, what capability that demonstrates, and how that capability may transfer into future opportunities.

---

## North Star

> MATE does not translate military terminology. MATE translates the capability, responsibility and trust that military terminology represents.

Military experience is often described through appointments, qualifications, ranks, courses and terminology that have meaning within the Armed Forces but little meaning outside them.

The Capability Interpretation Engine exists to uncover the capability hidden within that experience and explain it in a way that is understandable, evidence-based and useful.

---

## Why Traditional Translation Fails

Traditional military transition tools often focus on translating military terms into civilian equivalents.

**Examples:**

| Military | Traditional Translation |
|----------|------------------------|
| Section 2IC | Deputy Team Leader |
| Squadron Sergeant Major | Operations Manager |
| SA(90)A | Weapons Qualification |

Whilst factually correct, these translations often fail to communicate the capability, responsibility and trust represented by those roles.

Capability does not emerge from titles alone.

Capability emerges from what a person was **trusted to do**, **responsible for** and **how they performed**.

## Core Philosophy

The Capability Interpretation Engine is built upon the following principles:

### Understand Before Advising
Understanding must always come before recommendations.

### Capability Is Accumulated, Not Replaced
New experiences add to capability. They do not overwrite previous experience.

### Careers Are Remembered Through Postings
Military personnel naturally recall their careers through postings, appointments and assignments rather than abstract skills.

### Significant Experiences Reveal Hidden Capability
Deployments, exercises, temporary duties, specialist tasks and memorable events often reveal capability more effectively than formal role descriptions.

### Capability Emerges Through Corroboration
The strongest capability indicators emerge when multiple sources support the same conclusion.

### Confidence Must Be Explainable
Users should understand why conclusions have been reached and what evidence supports them.

### Absence Of Evidence Is Not Evidence Of Absence
Lack of documentary evidence does not automatically invalidate experience.

### Evidence Supports Understanding
Evidence strengthens capability understanding but does not act as certification.

### Interpretation Does Not Equal Certification
MATE interprets capability. MATE does not certify competence, regulatory compliance, fitness to practice or professional accreditation.

## Career Discovery Model

### Career Timeline

MATE discovers capability through career history rather than asking users to describe skills directly.

The Career Timeline captures:

- Postings
- Roles
- Responsibilities
- Deployments
- Significant Experiences
- Additional Duties

This approach reflects how military personnel naturally remember and discuss their careers.

### Capability Timeline

The user sees a **Career Timeline**.
MATE builds a **Capability Timeline**.

Capabilities are accumulated across:

- Postings
- Responsibilities
- Experiences
- Qualifications
- Evidence

This allows capability to be viewed as an evolving career narrative rather than a collection of isolated skills.

## The Capability Triangle

The Capability Triangle forms the primary reasoning model used by MATE.

Capability confidence is established through the corroboration of three elements.

```
        Evidence
           △
          / \
         /   \
        /     \
Responsibility — Behaviour
```

### Evidence
What objective evidence exists?

Examples: Qualifications, Certificates, Course Reports, SJARs, OJARs, Appraisals, Service Records, CVs, Uploaded Documents

### Responsibility
What was the individual trusted to do?

Examples: Leadership appointments, Welfare responsibilities, Training responsibilities, Operational responsibilities, Technical responsibilities, Command support roles

### Behaviour
How did the individual perform?

Examples: Course reports, SJAR comments, OJAR comments, Promotion recommendations, Instructor feedback, Performance observations, Narrative examples

> The strongest capability indicators occur when all three elements support the same conclusion.

---

## Evidence Types

### Documentary Evidence
Formal evidence provided through documents or records.

Examples: Qualifications, Certificates, Course Reports, SJARs, OJARs, Service Records

### Narrative Evidence
Evidence provided through conversation.

Examples: Experiences, Examples, Reflections, Stories, Situational discussions

Narrative evidence becomes particularly important when documentary evidence is unavailable.

---

## Confidence Model

The Capability Interpretation Engine does not assign arbitrary capability scores.

Instead, it establishes **confidence in capability understanding**.

Confidence is determined through:

- Evidence
- Responsibility
- Behaviour

Where confidence is low, MATE seeks further understanding rather than making assumptions.

> Low confidence should trigger **curiosity**. Not judgement.

## Reflection Framework

Once capability has been interpreted, MATE reflects understanding back to the user through five stages.

| Stage | Title | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 1 | What You've Done | Career history and experiences |
| 2 | What It Demonstrates | Capability interpretation |
| 3 | Where Evidence Is Strongest | Areas supported by multiple sources |
| 4 | Hidden Capability | Capabilities that may be under-recognised or undervalued |
| 5 | Areas For Further Exploration | Areas requiring additional evidence, reflection or discussion |

---

## Success Criteria

The Capability Interpretation Engine is successful when a user finishes the process with:

- Greater self-awareness
- Greater confidence
- Better understanding of their capability
- Clear visibility of transferable strengths
- Evidence-supported understanding
- A realistic appreciation of development needs
- Improved ability to communicate their value to others

> The outcome is not a skills report.
> The outcome is a clearer understanding of capability, responsibility and potential.
