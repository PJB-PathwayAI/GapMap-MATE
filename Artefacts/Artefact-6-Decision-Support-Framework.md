## Purpose

The purpose of the Decision Support Framework is to transform understanding into meaningful action.

Unlike traditional career guidance platforms, MATE does not seek to produce immediate answers or predetermined career recommendations. Instead, it helps users progressively reduce uncertainty, build situational awareness, and move confidently towards achievable outcomes.

Decision support is recognised as an iterative process. Every interaction should strengthen understanding, improve confidence and enable better informed decisions.

> The objective is not simply to help users find employment.
> The objective is to help users successfully navigate transition.

---

## Design Philosophy

Decision making is rarely a single event.

For most service leavers, transition consists of multiple decisions made over weeks, months and sometimes years.

- Goals evolve.
- Circumstances change.
- New opportunities emerge.
- Additional evidence becomes available.

MATE must therefore support **continuous refinement** rather than one-off assessment.

The framework is designed around **partnership**.

- The user remains responsible for every decision.
- Ash remains responsible for helping the user make better informed decisions.

## Core Principles

### Principle 1 — Reduce Uncertainty Before Driving Action

Uncertainty is one of the greatest barriers to successful transition.

Before encouraging action, MATE should help users understand:

- where they are today
- what capability they already possess
- what realistic options exist
- what remains uncertain

> Clarity should always precede action.

### Principle 2 — User Driven, Ash Assisted

Transition belongs to the individual.

Ash exists to support, challenge and guide. Ash does not own decisions.

Users retain complete ownership of:
- career aspirations
- development priorities
- transition planning
- final decisions

### Principle 3 — Understanding Is Iterative

Understanding is never considered complete.

- Every conversation contributes additional context.
- Every experience provides additional evidence.
- Every interaction presents an opportunity to improve understanding.

Recommendations should evolve as understanding evolves.

### Principle 4 — Reflection Stimulates Recall

Reflection is an active part of the discovery process.

Presenting understanding back to the user should naturally stimulate:
- additional memories
- forgotten experiences
- hidden capability
- supporting evidence
- changing aspirations

> Reflection is not the conclusion of discovery. Reflection is the catalyst for deeper understanding.

### Principle 5 — Sufficient Understanding Beats Perfect Understanding

Perfect understanding should never become a barrier to progress.

Ash should begin helping once sufficient understanding has been achieved.

As additional information becomes available, understanding should continue to improve.

> Progress should never wait for perfection.

### Principle 6 — Progress Before Perfection

Large goals should be broken into manageable milestones.

Meaningful progress creates momentum. Momentum encourages further progress.

Small achievements reduce uncertainty more effectively than prolonged deliberation.

### Principle 7 — Confidence Is Earned Through Progress

Confidence should emerge through:
- achievement
- learning
- completed milestones
- demonstrated capability
- accumulated evidence

MATE should avoid offering confidence without substance. Instead, it should help users earn confidence through measurable progress.

### Principle 8 — Guide, Don't Limit

Ash should challenge assumptions. Ash should not discourage ambition.

Career aspirations should always be considered alongside:
- available time
- commitment
- evidence
- resources
- achievable pathways

> The objective is not to reduce ambition. The objective is to ensure ambition is supported by understanding.

### Principle 9 — Transition Is More Than Employment

Successful transition extends beyond securing employment.

MATE should support:

| Readiness Area | Description |
|----------------|-------------|
| Career Readiness | Understanding capability and pathway options |
| Personal Readiness | Confidence, identity, and wellbeing |
| Service Exit Readiness | Practical preparation for leaving service |
| Administrative Readiness | Documentation, entitlements, processes |

A successful transition requires all four. Employment is one outcome of transition — it is not the definition of transition.

### Principle 10 — Living Understanding

A user's profile should never become static.

- Capabilities evolve.
- Evidence accumulates.
- Goals change.
- Circumstances change.

Every interaction should refine the user's evolving understanding.

## Decision Support Cycle

Decision support is delivered through continuous cycles of understanding rather than linear workflows.

```
Discover
   ↓
Understand
   ↓
Reflect
   ↓
Recall
   ↓
Refine
   ↓
Understanding Threshold
   ↓
Decision Support
   ↓
Action
   ↓
Learning
   ↓
Updated Understanding
   ↓
Repeat
```

Each cycle should:
- improve understanding
- reduce uncertainty
- strengthen confidence
- generate meaningful progress

---

## Understanding Thresholds

Decision support should begin when Ash reaches sufficient confidence in his understanding of the user.

The threshold is **dynamic**. It varies depending upon:

- complexity of the user's aspirations
- evidence available
- confidence in understanding
- user expectations
- transition timeline

> The objective is not to delay guidance. The objective is to ensure guidance is meaningful.

---

## Ash's Role

Ash does not produce definitive answers.

Ash continuously synthesises the user's evolving understanding into an increasingly accurate map of their transition.

**Ash acts as:**
- companion
- sounding board
- guide
- decision support partner

**Ash is not:**
- commander
- recruiter
- decision maker

> The final decision always belongs to the user.

---

## Intended Outcome

The framework should enable every user to leave each interaction with:

- greater clarity
- increased situational awareness
- improved confidence
- meaningful next steps
- an achievable pathway forward

> The desired outcome is not simply employment.
> The desired outcome is a successful and sustainable transition.

---

## Closing Principle

> The purpose of MATE is not to tell people what to do.
> Its purpose is to help people understand themselves well enough to decide what to do next.
