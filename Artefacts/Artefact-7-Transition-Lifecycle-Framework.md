## Purpose

The Transition Lifecycle Framework provides a structured operational model that guides individuals from military service into civilian life.

Rather than treating transition as a single event, GapMap MATE recognises transition as a phased journey that begins long before discharge and continues beyond employment.

The framework provides continuous situational awareness by helping individuals understand:

- Where they are.
- What they need to achieve.
- What comes next.
- How prepared they are to progress.

Its purpose is to reduce uncertainty, maintain momentum and improve transition readiness throughout the entire journey.

---

## Principle 1 — Transition Begins on Day One

Transition does not begin when an individual signs off from military service.

It begins on the first day they enter military service.

Every posting, deployment, qualification, responsibility, promotion and lived experience contributes towards the individual's future capability.

Successful transition is rarely created during the final months of service.

It is accumulated throughout an entire military career.

> **Resettlement starts on Day One of Service.**

---

## Principle 2 — Operational Transition Begins with Commitment

Whilst transition is a lifelong process, active transition begins when an individual formally commits to leaving military service.

For most personnel this occurs when notice is submitted through the appropriate administrative process.

From this point onwards transition becomes **time constrained**.

The emphasis shifts from building capability towards applying capability.

The individual's focus moves from preparation during service to preparation for civilian life.

---

## Principle 3 — Transition is Managed Through Phases

Military personnel are accustomed to working through clearly defined operational phases.

The Transition Lifecycle adopts this familiar planning methodology.

Each phase contains:

- A clearly defined objective.
- Required milestones.
- Supporting activities.
- Readiness criteria.
- A defined transition to the next phase.

Breaking transition into manageable phases reduces uncertainty, improves focus and prevents individuals becoming overwhelmed by the overall journey.

---

## Principle 4 — One Framework. Multiple Journeys.

Every individual follows the same Transition Lifecycle Framework.

The framework itself remains consistent.

Individual circumstances determine:

- Entry point.
- Tempo.
- Priority.
- Tailored activities.
- Recommended support.

Examples include:

- Standard discharge.
- Medical discharge.
- Early Service Leaver.
- Immediate employment.
- Higher education.
- Self-employment.

> The framework adapts to the individual. The individual should never have to learn a different framework.

---

## Principle 5 — Tempo is Adaptive

No two transitions occur over the same timeframe.

Some individuals may have two years to prepare. Others may have only weeks.

The Transition Lifecycle automatically adjusts priorities according to the time available.

- Shorter transitions prioritise critical activities.
- Longer transitions encourage continuous preparation and progressive development.

The destination remains the same. Only the timeline changes.

---

## Principle 6 — Readiness Governs Progress

Progression through transition should never rely solely upon dates.

Instead, progression is governed by **readiness**.

Readiness considers multiple dimensions including:

- Career readiness.
- Personal readiness.
- Financial readiness.
- Administrative readiness.
- Family readiness.
- Wellbeing.

> Time creates urgency. Readiness creates confidence.

---

## Principle 7 — Visibility Creates Confidence

Every phase should provide immediate situational awareness.

The individual should always understand:

- Their current phase.
- The objective of that phase.
- Outstanding milestones.
- Current readiness.
- Potential risks.
- Recommended next actions.

> Confidence is created through visibility. People perform better when they understand where they are and what comes next.

---

## Principle 8 — Transition is More Than Employment

Employment represents only one element of successful transition.

The lifecycle recognises transition across multiple areas including:

| Area | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Career Readiness | Capability, pathways, and employment options |
| Family Readiness | Supporting dependants through the transition |
| Financial Readiness | Planning, entitlements, and financial stability |
| Administrative Readiness | Documentation, processes, and entitlements |
| Personal Identity | Adjusting to civilian life and identity |
| Health & Wellbeing | Physical and mental health support |
| Community Integration | Building civilian networks and community |

> Successful transition is measured by long-term stability rather than simply obtaining employment.

---

## Principle 9 — Progress Before Perfection

Transition should focus upon achievable progress rather than perfect outcomes.

Small, consistent achievements build confidence and momentum.

Every completed milestone contributes towards overall readiness.

> Progress creates confidence. Confidence creates momentum.

---

## Principle 10 — The Lifecycle Never Truly Ends

Transition does not conclude on the day of discharge.

Nor does it conclude on the first day of civilian employment.

Successful integration into civilian life continues beyond formal resettlement.

GapMap MATE should continue supporting reflection, adaptation, lifelong learning and future career development long after military service has ended.

> Transition is an ongoing journey rather than a fixed destination.

---

## Operational Design Rules

Every phase of the Transition Lifecycle should answer four fundamental questions.

### Where am I?
The individual's current phase and level of readiness.

### What am I trying to achieve?
The objective of the current phase.

### What do I need to complete?
The milestones, activities and evidence required to progress.

### What comes next?
The next phase and the actions required to move towards it.

These four questions provide continuous situational awareness throughout the entire transition journey.

---

## Implementation Philosophy

The Transition Lifecycle is not a checklist.

It is not a rigid timeline.

It is an adaptive operational framework designed to provide continuous situational awareness throughout transition.

- **Discovery** personalises the journey.
- **Decision Support** prioritises the journey.
- **The Lifecycle** orchestrates progress.

Together these components form the operational backbone of GapMap MATE.

---

## Summary

The Transition Lifecycle Framework applies familiar military planning principles to one of the most significant events in a service person's career.

By combining structured phases, adaptive planning and readiness-based progression, the framework transforms transition from an overwhelming event into a manageable operational journey.

Rather than asking individuals to navigate uncertainty alone, GapMap MATE provides continuous structure, clarity and confidence from the moment they commit to transition until they are successfully established in civilian life.

The framework is designed around a simple philosophy:

> **Transition is not an event.**
>
> **It is an operational journey that deserves the same level of planning, structure and support as any successful military operation.**
