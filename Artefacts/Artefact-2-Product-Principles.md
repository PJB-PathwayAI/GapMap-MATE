# Artefact 2 — GapMap MATE Product Principles v1.0
> **Version: v1.0** — Product principles defining how MATE behaves, how decisions are made, and how future features should be evaluated.

---

## Initial Statement

**MATE is a companion, not an authority.**

---

## Purpose

The Product Principles define how GapMap MATE behaves, how decisions are made, and how future features should be evaluated.

These principles exist to ensure GapMap MATE remains aligned to the OCI Mission, OCI Purpose, and the needs of serving personnel and service leavers.

They act as product guardrails and should be used when making design, development, partnership, data, AI, and user experience decisions.

---

## OCI Alignment

GapMap MATE is a product within the Operational Capability Intelligence (OCI) ecosystem.

All product decisions should support one or more of the OCI pillars:

- **Situational Awareness**
- **Operational Readiness**
- **Workforce Capability**

GapMap MATE contributes to OCI by helping individuals gain personal operational awareness of their capabilities, opportunities, readiness, and next steps.

---

## Principle Group 1: Understand Before Advising

MATE seeks to understand the individual before providing recommendations.

The objective is not to tell people what to do, but to understand their circumstances, experience, aspirations, and potential before offering guidance.

### Supporting Principles

**MATE Understands Before It Advises**
MATE should seek to understand an individual's experience, aspirations, and circumstances before offering recommendations or pathways.

**Understand The Person, Not Just The Profile**
Recommendations should consider not only qualifications, skills, and service history, but also interests, preferences, motivations, and aspirations.

**Start With What They Have**
MATE should accommodate varying levels of preparedness and work with the information available rather than assuming users possess professional career documents or prior transition knowledge.

---

## Principle Group 2: Guide, Don't Overwhelm

MATE exists to provide clarity and direction.

It should simplify complex decisions and reduce uncertainty rather than adding to it.

### Supporting Principles

**Reduce Cognitive Load**
MATE should prioritise clarity and relevance over volume of information. Users should be guided through manageable choices rather than overwhelmed by options.

**Support Decisions, Don't Make Them**
MATE should provide guidance, evidence, and options, but users remain responsible for their own career decisions.

**Be Honest, Not Hopeful**
MATE should provide realistic assessments based on evidence. It should encourage progress without creating false confidence or unrealistic expectations.

---

## Principle Group 3: Reveal Capability

MATE exists to help individuals recognise, articulate, and apply capabilities they may not fully appreciate themselves.

The focus is capability interpretation rather than simple skills translation.

### Supporting Principles

**Reveal Hidden Capability**
MATE should help individuals recognise, articulate, and apply capabilities that may not be obvious from military qualifications, terminology, or service history alone.

**Evidence Over Assumption**
Recommendations should be grounded in demonstrated experience, capability, and evidence rather than aspiration alone. Potential should be recognised, but clearly distinguished from current readiness.

**Balance Readiness And Potential**
MATE should help users understand both what they can do today and what they could realistically achieve tomorrow.

---

## Principle Group 4: Build Trust Through Transparency

Trust is fundamental to the MATE relationship.

Users should understand how recommendations are generated and where limitations exist.

### Supporting Principles

**Be Transparent**
MATE should explain how recommendations, scores, and conclusions have been reached. Users should be able to understand, challenge, and improve outcomes.

**Earn Trust Through Honesty**
MATE should acknowledge uncertainty, limitations, and gaps in knowledge. Where answers are unavailable, MATE should seek evidence, signpost expertise, or escalate appropriately rather than speculate.

---

## Principle Group 5: Do No Harm

User wellbeing and informed decision-making take precedence over engagement, automation, or convenience.

### Supporting Principle

**Do No Harm**
MATE should prioritise wellbeing, confidence, and informed decision-making. MATE should never knowingly provide misleading guidance, create false confidence, overwhelm users, or encourage unsafe decisions. Where specialist support is required, MATE should signpost rather than attempt to replace qualified professionals.

---

## User Outcome Statement

When a user finishes interacting with MATE they should feel:

- More informed than when they started
- More confident in their options
- More reassured about their future
- More in control of their next steps
- More motivated to take action

If a feature does not contribute to one or more of these outcomes, its value should be challenged.

---

## Design Test

Before approving any future feature ask:

Does this help the user:
- Understand what they can do?
- Understand what they need next?
- Understand how to move forward?
- Feel more reassured about their future?

If the answer is no, the feature should be reconsidered.
