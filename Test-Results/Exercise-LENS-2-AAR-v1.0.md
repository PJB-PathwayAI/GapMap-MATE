## Assessment Against the Six Refinements

### 1. Reflection Pacing — PASS
Across the whole conversation, there was exactly **one full reflection** — the Capability Picture at the end, which is precisely where the spec says a full reflection belongs. Everything else was mini acknowledgements ("That's proper responsibility that, mate", "Right, got you", "Fair enough") woven into the next question. No Q→Reflect→Q→Reflect pattern.

### 2. Topic Completion Checkpoints — PASS
Three distinct topics (fire control, map reading, adventure training) were each closed out with a checkpoint before moving on — e.g. "Anything else on that one, or shall I ask about the map reading course?" Bodge closed two of them himself ("thaT WAS ALL ON THAT MATE"), which the conversation respected immediately rather than pushing for more.

Adventure training is the clearest test case: Bodge said "nothing really interesting," Smudge asked one genuine clarifying question (was he leading or part of the group), got the answer, and moved on without trying to manufacture significance where there wasn't any. This is the Evidence Rule's spirit applied to conversation — don't force a capability that isn't there.

### 3. Conversational Momentum — PASS
Chain questioning was used naturally ("what was that like, stepping up when Dave wasn't there? And you're doing fire control..."). A connection question also fired correctly — linking the map course mentoring moment back to earlier mentoring evidence ("Same thing as that lad in the shell scrape, isn't it"). Rhythm varied: some exchanges were single-line acknowledgements, others carried a genuine follow-up thread.

### 4. Smudge's Voice — PASS
Read-aloud test passes. Language stayed colloquial throughout discovery: "No way, mate, you kept that quiet," "Fair enough, doesn't have to be the highlight of your career." No therapy-speak, no "what I'm hearing is." The Capability Picture at the end was necessarily more structured — correctly so, since that's the designated translation point, not the discovery conversation itself.

### 5. Military Authenticity Before Civilian Translation — PASS
During discovery, no civilian/corporate terms were introduced ahead of the user. "2IC," "fire control," "map reading course," "night nav" — all terms Bodge used first or standard military terms he'd recognise. Civilian translation (quality assurance, resource management, technical planning) appeared only in the Capability Picture, as designed.

### 6. Natural Discovery (Seventh Principle) — PASS
The conversation moved at Bodge's pace, not a script's. When he said he was ready to see the Capability Picture, we moved straight there rather than padding with more questions. His own words afterward: **"no thats lot better than the last report cheers Smudge."** Unprompted, comparative, positive.

## Engineering Observation (non-blocking)

One inconsistency worth logging: during the conversation, Smudge referenced Bodge's earlier "shell scrape" mentoring story (from Exercise MIRROR) to draw a connection — but that specific anecdote was never written into *this* profile's evidence_log, since this was a fresh test profile for Exercise LENS 2. The connection was true (Paul/Bodge did say it in a previous exercise) but not traceable in this profile's own evidence trail.

This isn't a failure of the conversation — it's a reminder that **conversational memory and evidence provenance are not automatically the same thing.** If Smudge references something in conversation, and it matters to a capability claim, it needs to actually land in the evidence_log for that profile — not just live in Smudge's recollection of an earlier session. In this case the underlying capability (Peer Mentoring) was still evidenced independently via barracks mentoring + the map course, so nothing was fabricated. But the specific reference should have been backed by a matching evidence entry.

**No engine change required.** This is a discipline note for Smudge: when drawing a connection to something said in a previous session, make sure it's actually captured in evidence_log for the profile in play — don't rely on conversational memory alone.
