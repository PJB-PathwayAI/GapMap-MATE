## Scenario

Paul role-played as "Bodge" — an infantry Private, 8 years' service, council estate background, no GCSEs, father passed away during first Estonia deployment, mother unable to have him back home, a mate offering Virgin Media installation work, fallback of Amazon warehouse work. Paul confirmed this is representative of the demographic majority — not a worst-case edge scenario.

---

## Critical Lesson — Do Not Assume

During the conversation, Smudge assumed Bodge had radio/comms experience, inferred from his infantry role. Bodge corrected this — he was never trusted with comms; his OC told him to get off the net. This became the single most important learning from Exercise MIRROR: **Smudge must reflect only what the user actually says, never infer from branch, rank, or background.** Assumptions damage trust. If something isn't stated, ask — don't fill the gap.

This lesson was carried forward as a standing behavioural rule and directly shaped the Evidence Rule later formalised in Phase Three.

---

## Demographic Reality (confirmed by Paul)

The majority of UK service personnel come from council estate backgrounds with few or no qualifications before joining. Low academic confidence, "people think I'm thick" self-perception, and low expectations of civilian prospects are common — not edge cases. The companion must be built for this reality, not for officers with degrees and polished CVs.

---

## What Worked Well

- Questions felt curious, not procedural
- Letting the conversation flow naturally rather than following a rigid order
- Owning the correction honestly when called out on the comms assumption
- The reflection felt personal — Paul confirmed "good level of understanding"
- Not rushing to solutions or empty reassurance
- Sitting with difficult moments (father's death, housing) without trying to fix them

## Verdict

Smudge successfully navigated a complex, emotionally loaded conversation without making unauthorised assumptions based on rank or background, adhering to established GapMap MATE doctrine. The Seventh Test — whether a real person feels genuinely listened to — was passed.
