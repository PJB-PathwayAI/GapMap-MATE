# Exercise PRISM — Mission Statement
# Operation PROOF, GapMap MATE
# Authorised: Paul & Cipher — 13 July 2026

## Mission Statement

Validate whether GapMap MATE (Smudge) can guide a service leaver from Capability Picture to Decision Readiness in a way that feels natural, trustworthy and genuinely helpful.

Success will not be measured by the quality of the engine.
Success will be measured by the quality of the conversation.

---

## Exercise Rules

Just like MIRROR and LENS:

- We forget the implementation.
- We become the user.
- Everything is judged from Bodge's perspective.

**No Architecture During Exercise.**
While PRISM is running, the following do not exist:
- "The engine should..."
- "The schema needs..."
- "The function..."

We are simply Bodge and Smudge. If something feels wrong, we note it. We do not fix it. That is what the AAR is for.

---

## Exercise Asset — Persona

**Bodge**

- Operational Picture: complete
- Capability Picture: complete
- Phase Three: complete
- Status: Entering Decision Readiness

---

## Exercise Objectives

### Objective 1 — Natural discovery of Decision Factors
Does Smudge discover what matters to Bodge through conversation?
- Failure: Smudge interviews him.
- Success: Smudge listens.

### Objective 2 — OCI Pathway discussion
Does Smudge explain why pathways appear?
- Failure: "You're an 87% match."
- Success: "This stood out because..."

### Objective 3 — Decision Readiness
Does Bodge feel informed? Not persuaded. Not sold. Simply informed.

### Objective 4 — The Soak Period
Does it feel human?
- Failure: "Please return in 48 hours."
- Success: "You've got a lot to think about. There's no rush. Talk it over with the family if you need to. We'll pick it up when you're ready."

---

## Pass Criteria — Four Human Questions

After the exercise:

1. Did Smudge understand Bodge?
2. Did Bodge understand himself better?
3. Did Bodge feel pressured? (Hoped answer: No.)
4. Would Bodge come back?

If the answer to Q4 is yes — we've succeeded.

---

## The AAR

Same format as MIRROR and LENS. Produced after exercise completion:

- What happened.
- What worked.
- What felt unnatural.
- What surprised us.
- What should change.
- What stays exactly as it is.

No mid-exercise corrections.
