## Purpose

The Canonical User Questions define the minimum set of transition questions that GapMap MATE is designed to answer during the MVP.

They provide the architectural boundary for Decision Readiness and ensure every feature, conversation and engineering decision remains focused on genuine user need.

These questions are intentionally few in number.

They are not intended to represent every question a service leaver may ask.

Additional questions will only become canonical when supported by evidence gathered through Operation PROOF exercises, MVP trials and live user feedback.

## The Questions in Full

### 1. Who am I outside the military?

Help the individual understand their professional identity beyond rank, trade and service.

### 2. What am I actually good at?

Reveal demonstrated capability using evidence gathered throughout the conversation.

### 3. What skills have I developed that civilians will value?

Translate military experience into meaningful civilian capability without diminishing military identity.

### 4. Where could I realistically fit?

Identify realistic career directions that align with demonstrated capability and evidence.

### 5. What options do I have?

Present evidence-supported paths without prescribing a single outcome.

### 6. What gaps are stopping me today?

Identify capability, qualification, experience or confidence gaps that may affect transition readiness.

### 7. What matters most to me?

Understand the personal factors influencing transition decisions, including financial considerations, family, lifestyle, location, purpose and long-term aspirations.

### 8. Am I ready to make this move?

Support Decision Readiness by helping the individual assess both practical and personal readiness for transition.

### 9. What should I do first?

Provide a clear, manageable next step that creates momentum without overwhelming the individual.

### 10. Where can I get support along the way?

Help the individual identify appropriate organisations, resources and networks that can support their transition journey.

## Engineering Principle

Every significant feature within GapMap MATE should answer one or more Canonical User Questions.

If a proposed feature cannot be mapped to at least one Canonical User Question, it should be challenged before entering engineering.

---

## Operational Principle

The Canonical User Questions are not a questionnaire.

They are the questions the companion exists to answer through natural conversation.

They guide architecture rather than dictate conversation flow.

---

## Commander's Intent

GapMap MATE is not designed to answer every possible transition question.

It is designed to answer the most important transition questions well.

Quality of understanding takes precedence over quantity of functionality.

The Canonical User Questions establish the architectural boundary for the MVP and will evolve only through evidence gathered during Operation PROOF and live user feedback.
