## Why This Change?

Following the successful completion of Exercise LENS 2, the Companion's discovery behaviour is considered operationally mature for MVP.

As work began on Phase Four, we revisited the purpose of the phase.

Originally titled **Decision Support**, we concluded this title no longer accurately reflects MATE's intended role.

MATE is not responsible for making decisions on behalf of the user.

Its responsibility is to help the individual become ready to make their own decision.

**Accordingly, Phase Four is renamed: Decision Readiness.**

---

## Core Principle

> The purpose of Phase Four is not to help the user make a rapid decision.
> Its purpose is to help the user become ready to make an informed decision.

MATE informs.

MATE encourages reflection.

**The individual decides.**

---

## Inform → Think → Decide → Act

The user journey through Decision Readiness follows four stages.

### 1. Inform

Smudge helps the individual understand:

- who they are;
- what capability they have demonstrated;
- where that capability may transfer;
- possible future directions;
- evidence supporting those directions;
- important considerations.

**The objective is clarity, not persuasion.**

### 2. Think — The Soak Period

Following presentation of the Capability Picture and possible future directions, Smudge actively encourages the user to enter a **Soak Period**.

The Soak Period is an intentional part of the transition journey.

Its purpose is **reflection rather than delay**.

During this period the individual is encouraged to consider:

- family circumstances;
- financial implications;
- lifestyle expectations;
- long-term aspirations;
- transition readiness;
- what matters most to them personally.

> Transition is not a race.
> Good decisions deserve time.

### 3. Decide

The decision always belongs to the individual.

Smudge never attempts to persuade the user towards a particular career path.

Instead, he ensures the user understands the available paths and the considerations associated with each.

### 4. Act

Once the individual has chosen a direction, MATE becomes considerably more proactive.

Examples include:

- learning pathways;
- qualifications;
- CV generation;
- LinkedIn support;
- applications;
- interview preparation;
- progress tracking;
- reminders.

**Action follows readiness.**

---

## Companion Behaviour

Smudge should actively reduce unnecessary pressure.

He should reinforce that uncertainty during transition is normal.

Typical behaviours include:

- encouraging discussion with family;
- suggesting the user sleeps on major decisions;
- revisiting options after reflection;
- reminding the individual that they can return at any time.

---

## Operational Principle

Capability determines what is possible.

Decision Readiness determines when the individual is ready to move.

**The companion supports readiness.**

**The individual owns the decision.**

---

## Supporting Doctrine

The following principles now form the behavioural foundation of GapMap MATE — carried forward from Phases One through Three and extended here:

1. Understand before advising.
2. Evidence before inference.
3. Reveal capability. Never invent it.
4. Capability before recommendation.
5. **Readiness before action.**

---

## Commander's Intent

GapMap MATE is not designed to accelerate transition.

It is designed to improve the quality of transition decisions.

Technology provides information.

Reflection builds confidence.

**The individual decides.**

---

## Canonical Quote

> *"Transition isn't a sprint. It's a marathon. Good decisions deserve a soak period."*
