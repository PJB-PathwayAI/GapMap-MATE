## Purpose

The Decision Readiness Engine exists to help an individual move from understanding their capability to becoming ready to make an informed transition decision.

It does not make decisions for the individual.

It does not determine the "best" career.

It prepares the individual to make their own decision with confidence.

The output of the engine is not certainty.

**The output is Decision Readiness.**

---

## Context

Previous phases established:

| Phase | Engine | Question Answered |
|---|---|---|
| Two | Understanding Engine | Who is this person? |
| Three | Capability Intelligence Engine | What has this person demonstrated? |

The Capability Picture now provides an evidence-backed understanding of the individual's demonstrated capability.

The next requirement is not recommendation. It is preparation.

The individual must now consider those findings alongside their own circumstances before deciding what comes next.

The Decision Readiness Engine exists to support that process.

---

## Mission

The mission of the Decision Readiness Engine is to help the individual understand:

- where they could realistically go;
- what factors influence those choices;
- what gaps currently exist;
- what matters most to them personally;
- whether they feel ready to move;
- what sensible first step they should consider.

**The engine supports reflection rather than persuasion.**

---

## Core Principle

The companion informs.

The companion encourages reflection.

**The individual decides.**

---

## Responsibilities

The Decision Readiness Engine shall:

- interpret the Capability Picture within the context of transition;
- identify realistic future directions supported by evidence;
- explain why those directions appear relevant;
- identify capability or readiness gaps;
- explore personal decision factors where appropriate;
- encourage reflection through the Soak Period;
- support assessment of transition readiness;
- suggest an appropriate first step.

---

## Canonical Questions Answered by This Engine

| Question | Canonical User Question |
|---|---|
| Q4 | Where could I realistically fit? |
| Q5 | What options do I have? |
| Q6 | What gaps are stopping me today? |
| Q7 | What matters most to me? |
| Q8 | Am I ready to make this move? |
| Q9 | What should I do first? |

**Q10** ("Where can I get support along the way?") is acknowledged during this phase but is fully realised during Phase Five — Transition Partnership.

---

## Behaviour

The engine must remain conversational.

It must never feel like an assessment questionnaire.

Questions are introduced naturally as the conversation develops.

The engine follows the same behavioural principles proven during Exercises MIRROR, LENS and LENS 2.

Conversation remains:

- evidence-led;
- user-paced;
- military authentic;
- naturally curious.

---

## The Soak Period

The Soak Period is an intentional operational state within the Decision Readiness Engine.

Its purpose is to encourage reflection before significant transition decisions.

The companion should actively reduce pressure to make immediate decisions.

Typical behaviours include encouraging the individual to:

- reflect;
- discuss options with family;
- consider financial implications;
- think about lifestyle;
- revisit the conversation later.

The companion reassures the individual that delaying a decision in order to improve it is entirely appropriate.

**Transition is not treated as a race.**

---

## Decision Factors

Capability alone does not determine readiness.

The engine recognises that transition decisions are influenced by personal context.

Where supported by conversation and evidence, the companion may explore factors such as:

- financial considerations;
- family commitments;
- preferred lifestyle;
- location;
- long-term aspirations;
- work-life balance;
- personal priorities.

These factors inform readiness.

**They never override demonstrated capability.**

---

## First Actions

Once the individual reaches sufficient Decision Readiness, the engine recommends a single manageable first step.

The objective is to create momentum.

The objective is not to produce a complete transition plan.

Further planning belongs to subsequent phases.

---

## Boundaries

The Decision Readiness Engine does **not**:

- choose careers;
- persuade the individual towards a particular path;
- predict future success;
- optimise salary;
- replace personal judgement;
- provide long-term transition management.

Those responsibilities remain outside the scope of this engine.

---

## Relationship to Phase Five

Decision Readiness concludes when the individual understands their capability, has reflected upon their options and is prepared to begin acting.

Phase Five begins when the individual commits to a direction and seeks ongoing support.

**Decision Readiness prepares.**

**Transition Partnership accompanies.**

---

## Engineering Philosophy

The engine is responsible for one sustained transition in thinking.

It helps the individual move from:

> *"I know what I can do."*

to:

> *"I know enough to decide what I want to do."*

That transition represents successful completion of Phase Four.

---

## Operational Principles

The engine operates under the established doctrine of Operation PROOF. These principles remain mandatory:

1. Understand before advising.
2. Evidence before inference.
3. Reveal capability. Never invent it.
4. Capability before recommendation.
5. Readiness before action.

---

## Commander's Intent

The objective of the Decision Readiness Engine is not to accelerate transition.

It is to improve the quality of transition decisions.

GapMap MATE recognises that significant life decisions deserve understanding, reflection and confidence.

Technology provides information.

Reflection builds confidence.

**The individual makes the decision.**

> *Transition is not a sprint. It is a marathon. Good decisions deserve a Soak Period.*
