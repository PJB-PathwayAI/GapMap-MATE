## Purpose

An OCI Pathway is an evidence-backed representation of a potential civilian direction that an individual may realistically pursue based upon demonstrated capability.

OCI Pathways do not represent vacancies.

They do not represent employers.

They do not predict career success.

They provide a structured, explainable reference model that allows the Decision Readiness Engine to reason consistently about possible future directions.

---

## Context

| Phase | Engine | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Two | Understanding Engine | Who is this person? |
| Three | Capability Intelligence Engine | What has this person demonstrated? |
| Four | Decision Readiness Engine | What directions could they realistically explore? |

The Decision Readiness Engine requires a trusted reference model against which the Capability Picture and Decision Factors can be considered together.

**OCI Pathways provide that reference model.**

---

## Mission

The mission of an OCI Pathway is to describe a civilian direction in terms of capability rather than occupation.

Its purpose is to answer:

> *"What kind of future direction could someone with these demonstrated capabilities realistically explore?"*

---

## Core Principle

OCI Pathways represent **capability destinations**, not job titles.

They exist to support understanding rather than prescribe employment.

---

## Responsibilities

An OCI Pathway shall:

- describe a civilian direction using capability language;
- provide an explainable reference for Decision Readiness;
- support evidence-based matching;
- identify common transition expectations;
- remain independent of any individual user;
- evolve through evidence gathered during Operation PROOF and production use.

---

## Nature of an OCI Pathway

An OCI Pathway is **not**:

- a vacancy;
- a recruitment advertisement;
- a guaranteed outcome;
- a recommendation;
- a labour market prediction.

It is a structured representation of a recognised capability destination.

---

## Typical OCI Pathway Content

Each pathway should provide sufficient information to support explanation rather than exhaustive labour market intelligence.

Typical elements include:

| Element | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Pathway name | Consistent reference label |
| Purpose | What this direction represents |
| Civilian context | How this direction is understood in civilian employment |
| Capability profile | Demonstrated capabilities that align with this direction |
| Typical responsibilities | What individuals in this direction commonly do |
| Common civilian terminology | Language translation support for the individual |
| Typical entry routes | How service leavers commonly access this direction |
| Development opportunities | Growth and progression typical to this direction |
| Common transition gaps | Where capability alignment is frequently incomplete |
| Lifestyle considerations | Relevant factors (hours, travel, shift patterns, etc.) |
| Evidence source | Provenance of pathway information |
| Review status | Current standing (active / under review / retired) |
| Version history | Change record |

---

## Provenance

Every OCI Pathway shall have identifiable provenance.

The companion must always be able to explain:

- why the pathway exists;
- where its information originated;
- when it was last reviewed.

**OCI Pathways are curated organisational knowledge.**

**They are not generated dynamically by the companion.**

---

## Matching

The Decision Readiness Engine compares:

```
Capability Picture
    +
OCI Pathway
    through the lens of
Decision Factors
```

The resulting discussion should explain:

- why capability aligns;
- where uncertainty exists;
- which personal priorities influenced the conversation;
- what important gaps remain.

---

## Confidence Levels

OCI Pathways support three levels of conversational confidence:

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Matched Direction** | Supported through curated pathway information and demonstrated capability. |
| **Possible Direction** | Supported through partial capability alignment with recognised uncertainty. |
| **Worth Exploring** | Suggested through companion reasoning where sufficient evidence exists for discussion but insufficient evidence exists for stronger alignment. |

> Exploratory directions must always be clearly identified as such.
> They are never presented as equivalent to curated pathway matches.

---

## Evolution

OCI Pathways are expected to evolve.

- New pathways may be introduced when supported by sufficient operational evidence.
- Existing pathways may be refined as understanding improves.
- Obsolete pathways may be retired while retaining historical traceability.

**Operation PROOF governs all significant pathway changes.**

---

## Relationship to Decision Factors

OCI Pathways never determine suitability in isolation.

Personal priorities influence how pathways are discussed.

- Capability determines possibility.
- Decision Factors determine personal relevance.

---

## Relationship to Decision Readiness

OCI Pathways provide the structured knowledge required for Decision Readiness.

They do not produce decisions.

**They support informed reflection.**

---

## Boundaries

OCI Pathways do **not**:

- replace labour market research;
- recommend employers;
- guarantee employment;
- calculate salary;
- replace personal judgement;
- limit future exploration.

The companion remains free to discuss exploratory directions where appropriate, provided their confidence is communicated honestly.

---

## Operational Principle

OCI Pathways represent the organisation's current best understanding of recognised capability destinations.

They are organisational knowledge.

**Not immutable truth.**

---

## Engineering Principle

The pathway library should begin deliberately small.

Quality takes precedence over quantity.

New pathways are introduced only when supported by evidence.

The reference model should grow through operational learning rather than speculative completeness.

---

## Commander's Intent

OCI Pathways provide the bridge between demonstrated capability and informed transition decisions.

They exist to help individuals recognise where their capabilities may be valued without narrowing their future to a predetermined destination.

Every pathway should increase understanding.

None should remove choice.

> *Capability opens doors.*
> *OCI Pathways illuminate them.*
> *The individual chooses which one to walk through.*
